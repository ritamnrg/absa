The problem of how to design a database is commonly encountered by those not specifically trained and practiced in the art.
This book, therefore, is written with the lay person in mind.
In simple language, the author uses examples from her real-life experience to highlight the types of problems that can result from poor design, to motivate readers to do good design.
She then goes on to provide a sound method that readers can follow in order? to produce a good design
While focus is on implementing designs in relational databases, the author does not forget those readers who will choose to implement their database in a spreadsheet such as Microsoft Excel.
